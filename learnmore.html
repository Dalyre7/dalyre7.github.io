• Artifical Intelligence, ethical or inhumane?

Where do you see the tech industry in 30 years time? Many people imagine a cyber-punk world with robot wars and projected phones. Which isn’t a crazy theory, the possibility of AI being developed into society in a more predominant way is certainly not implausible. The idea of replacing jobs with AI has scared and enraged the public, however there are many elements to this.
If AI was able to be developed to replace army and police force (for example), this would mean less loss of human life and more efficient work. Of course there’s the opposing side of cost, malfunctions and of course, hacking. There really are endless possibilities in the tech and science industry, which is daunting in ways but admirable in most. Who really knows what extents
the emerging cyber-world will reach, or how long it will take before the world is entirely cyber.

How ethical is the emerging use of Artifical Intelligence?
Artificial Intelligence is one of the most prominent and controversial aspects to tech today. With a multitude of opinions and possibilities, the sheer existence of such invention has impacted the emergent of tech. Which brings us to ask, how ethical is the use of AI? The span of ‘ethics’ covers a vast range of different grounds, including human and environmental. Where we ask “how does this impact the planet, what is the environmental contribution to the use of AI” as well as “what is the impact of AI on society, how does this affect morals and human rights”.
